### LITERATURE

The **Fifth Edition of *Alcoholics Anonymous*** be approved with minor editorial changes. *(125–9)* Includes revised Appendix III (Medical View), Appendix V (Religious View), Appendix IV (Lasker Award footnote → aa.org), and Appendix VI (How to Get in Touch with A.A.).

The word **"Eskimo"** be removed from "Chapter 7: Working With Others" of *Alcoholics Anonymous* and replaced with a neutral, non-racial term. *(112–22)* Amendment to also include the *12 & 12* Tradition Eight essay failed at this stage *(50–82)* — but was later passed via Floor Action #6.

The committee noted that in accordance with the 2021 Conference Advisory Action authorizing development of the Fifth Edition *Alcoholics Anonymous*, which reads in part:

> "Pending its development, the draft Fifth Edition of the Big Book would include a new foreword, an updated preface and updates to expand on existing ideas in Appendices III and V"

"Appendix III — The Medical View on A.A." and "Appendix V — The Religious View on A.A." have been revised in keeping with the 2021 Conference Advisory Action.
"Appendix IV — The Lasker Award" was revised to include a footnote to direct readers to the awards page on aa.org.
"Appendix VI — How to Get in Touch with A.A." was revised to reflect more current best practices.

In keeping with the "A.A. World Services, Inc. Policy on Publication: Keeping A.A. Literature Current," which was accepted by the General Service Conference Advisory Action in 2020, and subsequently revised by the AAWS Board in 2022, the committee noted that the Publishing Department of the General Service Office will maintain editorial responsibilities for the Fifth Edition of the book *Alcoholics Anonymous*, including:

- Editorial "fine tuning" such as footnotes, punctuation, capitalization, spelling, page numbers, headings and subheadings
- Finalizing the titles and order of personal stories
- Editorial review of the title page, jacket copy and table of contents
- Coordinating the flow of work between the book designer, typesetters, copyeditors, proofreaders and the printers
- Designing the book cover and jacket
- Listing other A.A. literature in the final pages of the book
- Determining the potential inclusion of QR codes based upon current best practices as identified by AAWS, Inc.

### REPORT & CHARTER

Add to the FAQ in Chapter 1 **"What happens if a group decides not to hear a GSR report?"** of the **2026–2028 Edition** of *The A.A. Service Manual / Twelve Concepts for World Service*. The answer covers alternate sharing methods (with respect for Tradition Eleven) and the spiritual importance of two-way GSR communication. *(123–5 — recommit from 75th GSC)*

The answer reads:

> If a group decides not to hear a GSR report during a meeting, the information can still be shared in other ways. This may include a written report with printed copies, email, group messaging, a shared online document, or posting the report on a bulletin or noticeboard. When utilizing these methods, it is important to respect Tradition Eleven and protect anonymity, so that members' names are not publicly available.
>
> Hearing the GSR report is important because it helps keep the group connected to A.A. as a whole. As co-founder Bill W. explained, A.A. groups hold the ultimate responsibility for A.A. world services. The GSR serves as the communication link between the group and the larger Fellowship by sharing the group conscience with the Conference and bringing Conference actions back to the group. This two-way communication allows the group to have a voice in A.A., strengthens unity, and provides spiritual benefits by helping members stay informed, involved, and connected to the Fellowship beyond the home group.

Approve a draft of a new **"Amendments" section** for inclusion at the beginning of the Twelve Concepts for World Service section of the Service Manual. *(130–1)*
